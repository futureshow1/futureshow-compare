Most medical simulators live behind paywalls and PhD logins. The cardiologist who briefed me wanted something his residents could open in two clicks, on any laptop, during a corridor conversation — a working object, not a marketing demo.

CardioSim is what happens when a non-physician obsesses over that brief. Real Frank-Starling mechanics, real onset dynamics, real drug interactions — animated, observable, free, and stuck in a single HTML file you can email a student. It runs offline. It costs zero.

01 / Brief

What the cardiologist asked for.

Reviewer
ICU consultant
Warsaw teaching hospital

The brief was unusually concrete. Add to existing simulators: systemic and pulmonary resistance (SVR/PVR) in Wood Units, pressure-volume loops with real ESPVR, and a drug panel that simulates onset, not just steady-state effect.

"Educational simulators usually skip onset. But that's exactly what residents need to feel — that giving 200 mg metoprolol at 4 a.m. doesn't fix anything in the next thirty seconds." That sentence shaped the whole pharmacodynamic engine.

02 / Approach

Pharmacodynamics, verified.

Sources
Circulation
EJCTS
StatPearls
PMC

Every drug's HR / contractility / SVR / PVR / preload multipliers were sourced from primary literature, then reviewed for plausibility against typical ICU dosing curves. Onset modelled as a per-drug lerp constant (e.g. nitroglycerin reaches plateau in seconds; metoprolol takes minutes; amlodipine takes hours).

  • Metoprolol β-blocker
  • Carvedilol α/β-blocker
  • Diltiazem Calcium-channel blocker
  • Amlodipine Calcium-channel blocker
  • Nitroglycerin Vasodilator (preload↓)
  • Furosemide Loop diuretic
  • Dobutamine β1 inotrope
  • Milrinone PDE3 inotrope
  • Epinephrine α/β agonist
  • Norepinephrine α-vasopressor
03 / Outcome

Single HTML, two-click open.

Ship date
2026-Q2
Live on GitHub Pages
EN / PL

Final build: ~25 KB total, zero dependencies, offline-capable, single HTML file you can email. Resizable panels (cardiologist's screen is split 60/40 between heart view and waveforms; student's laptop is the reverse).

Eight clinical scenarios — normal sinus, A-fib, V-tach, STEMI, hemorrhagic shock, cardiac tamponade, pulmonary embolism, HFrEF — load with one click. Active drug panel tracks combined effects and surfaces real interaction warnings (e.g. metoprolol + diltiazem = additive AV block risk).

04 / Stack

What it's made of.

Tech
Vanilla JS
Canvas 2D
Web Audio (pulse oximeter)
No frameworks

Pure HTML/CSS/JS. Canvas 2D for the heart, waveforms, PV loops. Web Audio for the pulse oximeter beep. No build step, no framework. The whole thing is one file, version-controlled with the rest of the portfolio.

This matters because the customer is teaching hospitals and medical students. They don't want a Node install. They want a thing that works for ten years without maintenance. Single-file HTML is that thing.
