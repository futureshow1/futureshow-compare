Everyone buys A Thousand Plateaus. Almost nobody finishes it. The reason isn't that it's hard — it's that the format is wrong for the content. Deleuze and Guattari wrote what they called a rhizomatic book: read it from any plateau, in any order, return as often as you like. But it ships as a linear text of 600 pages.

The Schizoanaliza game gives the rhizome back its shape. Forty-five concepts as glowing nodes. Connections drawn between them. Pick a starting point — the Body Without Organs, the Anomalous, Deterritorialisation, the Three Social Machines — and walk.

01 / Brief

A 600-page book, flat.

For
Students of philosophy
Critical theorists
Artists working with Deleuze

Continental philosophy departments still teach Deleuze. Practising artists still cite him. Anti-psychiatry circles still build on him. But the books themselves are an obstacle — not because of difficulty, because of shape. They demand a graph reader and they're shipped as a chain.

Brief: build a graph reader. Forty-five nodes (the key concepts), edges (their actual textual relationships), and a UI that lets you do what the book asks you to do — read it as a rhizome, not a chain.

02 / Approach

Constellation. Axis. Machines.

Three views
Concept constellation
Argumentative axis
Social machines

The constellation — 45 nodes positioned by conceptual proximity, edges = textual links between concepts in Anti-Oedipus and A Thousand Plateaus. Click a node, open the concept page (definition, detailed essay, source quotes).

The argumentative axis — the linear "spine" of the books, from critique of psychoanalysis to ontology of becoming, so readers who want sequence can have it.

The three social machines — territorial, despotic, capitalist — mapped against history, with explanations of how each codes and decodes desire differently.

03 / Outcome

A place, not a text.

Use
University seminars
Reading groups
Self-study

The constellation has a growth animation — when you open the page, you see the rhizome literally building itself, nodes connecting to neighbours. The metaphor and the implementation are the same gesture.

Quiz layer: 50+ multiple-choice questions, distributed across concepts, with explanations citing the source text. A teacher can use Schizoanaliza in a 90-minute seminar; a self-learner can use it over a month of evening sessions.

Single HTML, ~180KB of JS for the concept dictionary and graph engine. Force-directed layout for the rhizome (gentle physics — nodes drift, push each other, you can drag them around).

All Polish text re-checked in 2026 with full diacritic restoration (2,300+ characters fixed in one pass). Quiz translations curated from Polish editions of Anty-Edyp and Tysiąc plateau.
