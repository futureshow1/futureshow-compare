Manifesto · 60 seconds

FutureShow is a studio for interactive narratives. We treat each project as an architecture problem: how do you make a difficult subject — pharmacology, oligarchic finance, post-structuralist philosophy — into something a person can play with, not just read about? The answer is rarely a longer article. It's almost always a different kind of object.

01
Medical Simulation 2026

A working pharmacology lab, in a browser tab.

Ten cardiovascular drugs, eight clinical scenarios, real-time hemodynamics — verified against Circulation, EJCTS, StatPearls.

Most medical simulators live behind paywalls and PhD logins. CardioSim is what happens when a non-physician obsesses over a cardiologist's brief: real Frank-Starling mechanics, real onset dynamics, real drug interactions — animated, observable, free, and stuck in a single HTML file you can email a student. It runs offline. It costs zero. It is being used.

Investigation Geopolitics 2026

Where the world's energy actually moves.

Seven interactive maps. 240+ pipelines, fields, LNG terminals, chokepoints. Status-aware: damaged, shut, active, proposed.

After 2022, every newsroom in Europe needed a way to talk about gas flows that wasn't either a Reuters wire or a Stratfor PDF. The Atlas is the third option: open, source-cited, status-aware. Hover any line; see who built it, who shut it, what year, what for. Geopolitics through what's actually on the ground.

Philosophy Education 2026

Anti-Oedipus, navigable.

45 key concepts of Deleuze & Guattari mapped as a rhizomatic constellation. 50 quiz questions. Three social machines.

Everyone buys "A Thousand Plateaus." Almost nobody finishes it. The interactive constellation lets you walk the concepts as a graph rather than a 600-page sequential text: pick a node (the Rhizome, the Body without Organs, the Anomalous), see what it connects to, why, and what it costs. Philosophy made into a place.

05
Art Editorial 2026

A dodecahedron you can walk into.

Sectio Aurea — an interactive 3D atlas of the Golden Ratio. Twelve chambers, seven playable rooms, gold-and-rank progression. From Matila Ghyka's 1931 treatise to a walkable, modern object.

Ghyka's Złota Liczba is a 400-page treatise on number, proportion, beauty and cosmos. Practically unreadable end-to-end. Sectio Aurea reorganises it as a 3D dodecahedron-navigator: each of the twelve pentagonal faces opens onto a chamber. Seven of them are fully playable — Number (audio), Proportion, Pentada (shader), Solids, Garden, Lodge (constellation), Probierz (myth-game) — with a gold currency, ranks, and badges. Philosophy of beauty, finally walkable.

Investigation Big Tech 2026

The Big Tech scandal index.

33 documented cases across platform misconduct, privacy breaches, antitrust, labour practice. Searchable, comparative, source-cited. PL/EN.

The next person writing a thesis on platform power shouldn't have to start from page one of every story. Matryca is the index that doesn't exist anywhere else: each case mapped to its evidence, its outcome, its repeating pattern. Built once, maintained forever. Free to cite.
