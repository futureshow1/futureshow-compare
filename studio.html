FutureShow is a deliberate experiment in scale: how much can one experienced author ship when the assistant is an LLM rather than a junior dev? The answer turned out to be: a flagship every two months, indexed and cited.

Each project starts from a real institutional brief — a teaching hospital, a press desk, a philosophy seminar, a policy NGO. It ends as a working object, served from GitHub Pages, free to use, free to fork, written to last ten years without a build chain.

02 / Approach

Four rules.

01

Direct

No agencies, no account layers. The person who writes the code is the person who reads your brief.

02

Browser-native

HTML, CSS, vanilla JS, Canvas, three.js. No app store. No login wall. Works on the laptop you already have.

03

Yours to keep

Static files, in your repo, with your domain. No SaaS lock-in. No invoice in the second year for the same thing.

04

Fast to ship

Brief to working prototype in days, not months. AI as partner compresses the cycle; the editorial judgment stays human.
