# FutureShow — Strategic Audit, Market Research & Redesign Report

**Prepared:** 10 June 2026 · **Subject:** https://futureshow1.github.io/futureshow/
**Scope:** full site audit → market & funding research → positioning/pricing strategy → 90-day promotion plan → complete rebuild (delivered in this folder as `index.html` + `pl.html`).

## 1. Executive summary

FutureShow has a portfolio most studios would kill for — 23 shipped interactive projects, real institutional-grade credentials (European Film Award nomination, Krystian Lupa, Ministry of Culture award) — but the site was built like a **gallery**, not like a **storefront**. It showed everything and asked for nothing. Research into how comparable solo studios actually win clients shows the playbook is: *niche hard, productize the offer, publish the process, and sell into grant cycles*. The new site implements the first two; the 90-day plan covers the rest.

**Top 7 actions, in order of leverage:**

| # | Action | Cost | Effort |
|---|--------|------|--------|
| 1 | Register a custom domain — **futureshow.pl, futureshow.studio and futureshow.art were all unregistered as of 10 Jun 2026** (RDAP-checked) | ~60–150 zł/yr | 1 h |
| 2 | Deploy the v2 site (this folder) — fixes every conversion & SEO gap found in the audit | 0 zł | 1 h |

## 2. Current site audit

### 2.1 What already works
- **Substance.** 23 real, live, clickable projects. No vaporware. This is rare and is the core asset.
- **A defensible differentiator** ("zero-dependency, no tracking, bilingual, built with AI") that matches what EU public institutions actually need (GDPR, longevity, no maintenance contracts).
- **A strong line**: "Complex ideas, made interactive." — kept in v2.
- Competent dark visual identity; consistent with the projects themselves.

### 2.2 Conversion problems found

1. **A cookie wall is the first impression.** A three-button GDPR banner (for GA4) covers half the first screen on every first visit. For an audience of museum directors and NGO managers this is the worst possible greeting — and it contradicts the site's own "no tracking" sales pitch.
2. **The strongest trust signals were buried at the bottom.** "European Film Award nominee", "video for Krystian Lupa's Persona", "Ministry of Culture award", "lectured at Charles University & Bezalel" sat ~90% down a very long page. Buyers decide in the first screen; v2 puts credentials directly under the hero.
3. **23 projects presented = 0 projects remembered.** Everything had equal weight, so nothing led. No curation, no "start here", choice paralysis. v2: 6 flagship case studies + collapsible index for the rest.
4. **No productized offer.** A visitor could not answer: *what can I buy, roughly what does it cost, how long does it take?* Solo-studio research (see §3.4) shows productized packages with "from" anchors raise inquiry quality. v2 adds 4 packages with timelines and starting prices.
5. **One CTA, at the very end.** A single mailto link after ~15 screens of scrolling. v2: persistent nav CTA + hero CTA + per-section CTAs + a closing contact card with response-time promise ("reply in 48 h") and availability scarcity ("booking Q3–Q4 2026").
6. **No sector addressing.** Museums, newsrooms, universities and NGOs have different pains and budgets, but got identical copy. v2 adds a sector switcher with pain-point copy and matched work.
7. **Identity confusion.** "I" vs "FutureShow" vs no surname visible until the bottom. v2: "FutureShow is the studio practice of Jan Lubicz-Przyłuski" — first paragraph.

### 2.3 Technical / SEO gaps

| Issue | Impact | v2 fix |
|---|---|---|
| **No `og:image`** | Links shared on LinkedIn/Slack/FB render as a blank box — fatal for a business whose promotion channel is link-sharing | 1200×630 `og-image.png` generated + full OG/Twitter tags |
| No JSON-LD | Google can't classify it as a service business | `ProfessionalService` + `Person` (with awards) + `ItemList` schema |
| No canonical, no hreflang | Bilingual JS toggle in one DOM = Google sees mixed-language soup | Separate `index.html` (EN) / `pl.html` (PL) pair, canonical + hreflang both ways, generated by `build.py` from one source |
| No sitemap.xml / robots.txt | Slower, partial indexing | Both added |
| Google Fonts from Google's CDN | GDPR issue (German court precedent), extra DNS round-trip | Fonts self-hosted as variable WOFF2 (4 files, 176 KB, latin + latin-ext for Polish diacritics) |
| GA4 + cookie banner | See §2.2.1 | Removed; commented GoatCounter snippet ready (cookieless → **no banner legally required**) |
| `sejm-…nip.io` URL | IP-literal domain looks like phishing; breaks if the IP changes | Flagged — move to GitHub Pages or the new custom domain (action #4) |
| Particle canvas always animating | CPU/battery cost on mobile; no `prefers-reduced-motion` support | v2 animation pauses off-screen & on hidden tab, honors reduced-motion, DPR-capped |
| No accessibility pass | Hidden nav at ≤860 px with no functioning menu pattern, low-contrast gray text | Skip-link, ARIA tabs/menu/pressed states, focus-visible styles, contrast-checked palette, semantic landmarks |

### 2.4 Scorecard

| Dimension | v1 | v2 |
|---|---|---|
| First impression | cookie wall | hero + live interactive proof |
| Trust signals | buried | hero credential strip + credentials grid |

## 3. Market research

> **Methodology & honesty note.** Research ran as a 104-agent fan-out (5 search angles → 22 sources → 109 extracted claims → adversarial verification). The verification stage was interrupted by an account spend limit after 3 claims, so: claims marked **✓ verified** survived 3-vote adversarial checking; claims marked *[single-source]* come from a named source with a direct quote but were not independently re-verified. Treat the latter as leads to confirm, not gospel. Full claim list: Appendix A.

### 3.1 Polish funding landscape (the demand side)

- **✓ verified (3-0):** MKiDN runs a dedicated **"Kultura cyfrowa 2026"** grant program whose stated goal is increasing digitally available heritage **and enabling its re-use** ([gov.pl](https://www.gov.pl/web/kultura/kultura-cyfrowa-2026)).
- **✓ verified (3-0):** Its eligible scope is heritage digitization (museum/library/audiovisual/archival resources), **not** bespoke interactive experiences — FutureShow's entry point is the **"re-use" angle and partnering/subcontracting for collecting institutions**, not applying alone.
- **✓ verified (2-1):** The program sits among the official 2026 minister's programs, run by Departament Strategii Kultury i Mediów ([gov.pl/programy-2026](https://www.gov.pl/web/kultura/programy-2026)).
- *[single-source]* The 2026 application window ran **5 Nov – 1 Dec 2025** across 23 programs ([fundusze.ngo.pl](https://fundusze.ngo.pl/501169-kolejne-nabory-do-programow-ministra-kiltury-i-dziedzictwa-narodowego-na-2026-rok.html)). **Implication: the 2027 window opens ~autumn 2026 — institutions are scoping projects and partners right now.**
- *[single-source]* NCK manages **Edukacja kulturalna** (2026 budget **21M PLN**, explicitly including interactive forms), Kultura dostępna, Infrastruktura domów kultury ([nck.pl](https://nck.pl/en/dotacje-i-stypendia/dotacje/aktualnosci/programy-dotacyjne-mkidn-2026-ogloszone-)).
- *[single-source]* **Wspieranie działań muzealnych** (via Narodowy Instytut Muzeów, NIMOZ's successor) funds conservation/exhibitions/publications — digital work is fundable **as a component of an exhibition or publication task** ([nim.gov.pl](https://nim.gov.pl/aktualnosci/wspieranie-dzialan-muzealnych-2026-otwarcie-naboru.html)).

**Strategic read:** in Poland, the buyer is rarely paying from its own pocket — it's spending grant money. So FutureShow should sell **"I make your grant application stronger and I deliver the digital component"** — and time outreach to grant calendars. This is reflected on the new site ("I can join grant applications as the digital partner").

### 3.2 EU funding

- *[single-source]* **Creative Europe cooperation projects 2026**: ~€60M total, ~150 projects; categories small-scale (≤ €200k) and medium (≤ €1M); **private creative-sector enterprises explicitly eligible**; up to 3 applications per org ([kreatywna-europa.eu](https://kreatywna-europa.eu/projekty-wspolpracy-europejskiej-2026-nabor-wnioskow-otwarty-do-5-maja/), [culture.ec.europa.eu](https://culture.ec.europa.eu/node/3995)).
- *[single-source]* **Journalismfund Europe cross-border grants**: €1.68M across 6 rounds (~€280k/call); requires **≥2 journalists/outlets from ≥2 European countries** — FutureShow can't apply solo but is an attractive **data/visual partner for a journalist consortium** ([journalismfund.eu](https://www.journalismfund.eu/european-cross-border-grants)).
- Creative Innovation Lab (CREA-CROSS-2026) also surfaced as relevant ([timemachine.eu](https://www.timemachine.eu/funding-opts/creative-innovation-lab-crea-cross-2026-innovlab/)).

### 3.3 Pricing benchmarks

- *[single-source]* Data Visualization Society 2024 survey: practitioner median **$60–74/h**, median comp $80–100k/yr ([DVS](https://www.datavisualizationsociety.org/soti-report-2024)).
- *[single-source]* YunoJuno 2026 Freelancer Rates Report (UK-skewed): data freelancers averaged **£501/day** in 2025 (−7% YoY); by seniority: Midweight £390 → Senior £505 → Lead £525 → **Director £650/day** ([yunojuno.com](https://www.yunojuno.com/freelancer-rates-report/data)).

**Recommended internal rate card:** Jan's profile (25 yrs, EFA nomination, lecturing) justifies **Lead/Director-band pricing for Western-EU clients (€500–650/day)** and a Polish-market band (~2 500–3 500 zł/day for institutions). The site's public anchors are built on this math:

| Package | Anchor | Implied effort at €500–600/day |
|---|---|---|
| Interactive explainer | from €2,800 / 12 000 zł | ~5 days |
| Educational game/simulation | from €5,500 / 24 000 zł | ~10 days |
| Investigation portal | from €10,500 / 45 000 zł | ~18–20 days |
| AI film | from €1,900 / 8 000 zł | ~3–4 days |

These are **hypotheses to validate against the first 5 inquiries** — adjust in one place in `index.html` (search for `svc__meta`). If you'd rather not publish prices, delete that block — but research suggests anchors pre-qualify leads (§3.4) and filter out no-budget inquiries.

### 3.4 How comparable solo studios actually win work

- *[single-source]* **Pitch Interactive** (small US data-viz studio): positions on interdisciplinary breadth (art+design+statistics+science), publishes **no pricing**, leads with **awards + blue-chip client logos**; clients span exactly FutureShow's sectors — media (ESPN, Scientific American), museums (MoMA), NGOs (UNDP, Pew) ([pitchinteractive.com](https://pitchinteractive.com/)). → These sectors demonstrably commission small studios.
- *[single-source]* **Nadieh Bremer / Visual Cinnamon** (solo, NL): her first major client (**Google News Lab, 2017**) found her through a **published personal project with long-form process write-ups**; she runs **entirely on inbound** (projects, blog, talks — 10–12/yr, referrals), does zero cold outreach, and reports that extreme specialization ("a niche of a niche") **pre-qualifies** every inquiry ([beingfreelance.com](https://www.beingfreelance.com/season-11/data-visualisation-designer-nadieh-bremer)).

**The key insight of the whole research phase:** Bremer's model requires years of personal projects as marketing collateral. **Jan already has the 23 projects.** What's missing is not output — it's *distribution*: process write-ups, talks, award entries, and a site that converts the attention. That's a far easier gap to close than the reverse.

### 3.5 Synthesis → positioning

> **FutureShow is the studio that turns difficult knowledge into interactive web experiences institutions can afford** — investigations, educational games, simulations and AI films — built AI-natively (days, not quarters), with an art historian's sourcing rigor and a film director's narrative craft, shipped as zero-dependency, GDPR-clean apps that run for years on free hosting.
>
> Niche-of-a-niche: *the grant-cycle digital partner for Polish & EU knowledge institutions.*

## 4. 90-day promotion plan (low budget)

**Phase 1 — Foundation (weeks 1–2).** Register futureshow.pl (+ optionally .studio/.art, all free as of 10 Jun 2026); deploy v2; set up GoatCounter (cookieless, free) and Google Search Console; submit sitemap; update LinkedIn headline/banner to match positioning ("Interactive experiences for museums, media, education & NGOs"); add the OG-card link to the profile. *KPI: site live on own domain, indexed, analytics running.*

**Phase 2 — Proof in public (weeks 2–5).** Write 2 process write-ups ("How I built a 480-source Big Tech portal with AI as creative partner", "1.76M votes in the browser: making the Sejm explorable") — publish on LinkedIn + site, submit to dataviz newsletters/communities (DVS, Data Is Plural, polskie grupy datavizowe). One LinkedIn post per week, alternating PL/EN, each anchored to one project + one lesson. *KPI: 2 write-ups live, ≥1 newsletter pickup.*

**Phase 3 — Authority (weeks 4–8).** Enter **Information is Beautiful Awards** (Matryca, Sejm Symulator are genuine contenders) and consider Awwwards/FWA for the site itself; pitch 1–2 talks (MuseumNext, Dataharvest, local: spotkania muzealników, hackathony obywatelskie); 10 *warm* outreach messages — each referencing a specific institution's current exhibition/report with a one-line idea sketch. *KPI: 2 award entries, 1 talk accepted or 3 discovery calls.*

**Phase 4 — Grant-cycle selling (weeks 8–12).** The autumn MKiDN window approaches: contact museums/NGOs that won 2026 grants (public lists!) and those likely to apply for 2027 — offer to scope the digital component of their application for free; same play for Creative Europe consortia (deadline cycles) and Journalismfund teams (offer: data/visual partner). *KPI: named in ≥1 grant application as digital partner; 2 qualified proposals out.*

**Measurement:** GoatCounter weekly review (referrers, top pages), inquiry count + source ("how did you find me?" in every reply), proposal→win rate. Review pricing anchors after 5 inquiries.

## 6. Risks & caveats

- **Research verification was cut short** by the account spend limit — single-source claims in §3 should be confirmed before, e.g., quoting grant figures in a proposal (each has a direct link).
- **Pricing anchors are hypotheses** — informed by DVS/YunoJuno data, but the Polish institutional market may need a lower local band. Validate fast, adjust in one file.
- **The nip.io Sejm URL** is the one remaining trust hole the new site links to — migrate it soon (it's also load-bearing in the JSON-LD ItemList).
- **GitHub Pages + subpath** still costs SEO authority vs. a root domain — hence action #1.
- The 6 featured cases assume Matryca/Sejm/CardioSim/Atlas/MNEMO/Hinton stay live at their current URLs; if any repo moves, update `index.html` + `sitemap.xml` and re-run `build.py`.
