Hungry Artist/Fe, version 2005/2015

video, blackboard

"Before speaking further about culture, I must remark that the world is hungry and not concerned with culture, and that the attempt to orient toward culture thoughts turned only toward hunger is a purely artificial expedient. (…) What is most important, it seems to me, is not so much to defend a culture whose existence has never kept a man from going hungry, as to extract, from what is called culture, ideas whose compelling force is identical with that of hunger." Antonin Artaud, Theater and Its Double

Exhibitions: Cork Artists Collective Residency Program, Guest House Gallery, Ireland

Pictures from Ukraine - Water/Fire

HD video loop

"Pictures from Ukraine" is a visual commentary on the representation of revolution and conflict in relation to elementary forces. These forces equally rule collectives and individuals. Real footage from Ukraine sent to Jan by Maidan activists was transformed into this minimalist video on the body of elements. Curator Inez Ruiz Artola, wrote "(...) su vídeo "Agua y fuego: paisaje de Ucrania" que cerraba la muestra con una nota minimalista, elegante (el agua) y al tiempo llena de la potencialidad expresiva del fuego (los medios, Ucrania…los conflictos)."

Exhibitions: Arte Actual de Polonia, ARTE KALEA, Balmaseda, Basque Country

LUPA LOOP 2, (Above/Below)

video 17'20''

Interrelation of two series of video shots: the body of a woman versus the body of a doll/a model, both under a force of male gaze. The instalation is focused on a relation between two images, that of an actress during photo session and that of a one-to-one model, effigy of her body, prepared to be burned. Double role of a "photographer" who - with the use of a shutter-trigger - changes place of projections, from above to below and vice versa. Changing hierarchy, he swaps what is alive in the place of the dead, and back.

Exhibitions: In Progress, Ars Cameralis Festival, Katowice; Cork Artists Collective, Cork;

The Grandmaster

35 mm, 5 min

Fisherle lives under the bed of a young whore. He is fascinated by chess, listens to the radio transmissions of the games. Suddenly one day, as a client to the prostitute arrives the great Capablanca in his own person. The Game starts, the game for everything. Never finished film of Daniel Bauman, inspired by motives from Elias Canetti's "Auto-da-fe", was reorganized and reedited by Jan. This work was a inception, a commencement for a multiannual project of elevating the art of film editing to a major role in creation, called Postdirection.

Exhibitions: Swedenborg House, London;
